INAUGURATION OF PES SB CET AND WORKSHOP ON ‘HOW TO WRITE AND PRESENT TECHNICAL PAPERS

Power and Energy Society

Today Power and Energy Society of IEEE was introduced in IEEE SB CET. The inauguration
ceremony commences with the welcome speech of Hrithu O A, Vice Chair of PES, SB CET. Er.
Harindarlal, chairman of IEEE PES Kerala Section lighted the lamp in the presence of Er. Biju,
Secretary of IEEE PES Kerala Section, Prof. P Sreejaya, staff in charge of IEEE PES SB CET and
Mr. Jerrin George James, chairman of IEEE SB CET. The future plans of our student chapter was
proposed by Mr. Praveen T A, chairman of PES, SB CET. Ms. Anusree T K, Secretary of PES, SB
CET felicitated the inauguration ceremony.

The inauguration ceremony was followed up by a workshop on ‘How to write and present
technical papers’. The workshop started with the introduction of Dr. Francis M Fernandez, senior
faculty of Electrical department, CET. The session was handled by Mr.K Chandramohan. He
explained key attributes and basic layout of a technical paper. Also illustrated some common
mistakes that might confront while preparing a technical paper and their corrections. During the
workshop the participants were made to modify their technical paper which they already
prepared according to the evaluation of a panel of judges. The workshop was concluded at 4:30
pm with the vote of thanks by Er.Biju, Secretary of IEEE PES Kerala Section.

Mock Placement Drive 17

WIE

 

IEEE WIE CET Chapter conducted the Mock Placement Drive 17 for all second year students to give them an experience on the placement process and to help them equip better with the skills needed to emerge successful in it.The competition, conducted on Aug 11, witnessed a participation of 35 enthusiastic students for the preliminary round which covered questions from subjective,logical reasoning, aptitude and general knowledge areas.All the participants were given the chance to attend the Group Discussion as this would help them assess their communication skills.They were divided into different groups and various topics were discussed.The event was conducted by third year IEEE members.

 

Based on their performance in both the rounds, 15 bright contestants were shortlisted for the final round.The interview was conducted by fourth year IEEE members Jerrin, Amal,Sai Lekshmi and Adarsh. At the end, AbhijithNandagopal of Computer Science Department and Mathew Chetipuzha of Electrical Department emerged the ‘IEEE Emerging Student of the Year 17 ‘ while Mathew George of Computer Science Department bagged the consolation prize.

IEEE CS – IEEE Xtreme Practice 1

Computer Society

The IEEE Computer Society held its weekly session on August 8th 2017. The prime event of the session, the IEEEXtreme Coding Practice, was handled by Nirmal. Participants of the coding practice were made to sign up on HackerRank to take the contest. There were a set of 7 questions, that were either of easy or medium difficulty level, prepared by Nirmal. The students keenly tried cracking the problems. Some of the senior IEEE members helped the students throughout the contest. The coding practice lasted for about an hour. The solutions of each question were explained and doubts were cleared. The main aim of the session was to introduce the students to the various strategies of coding competitions for cracking even the toughest of problems. This was satisfactorily fulfilled within the 2 hour session.

Before concluding, the general rules and procedures of programming competitions like the IEEEXtreme were discussed. The session drew to a close at around 6.15pm.
